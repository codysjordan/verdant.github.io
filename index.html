Hannah Maharrey, M.P.A.

Hannah Maharrey is a public administration professional with an emphasis in nonprofit leadership. She is passionate about grassroots organizations and community based nonprofits. She established Verdant Consulting to support nonprofits and facilitate the sustainability and adaptability of grassroots movements within their local communities.

Hannah has domestic and international nonprofit experience. She is from North Mississippi and has a Bachelor’s degree in Journalism from the University of Mississippi, but her dedication to volunteering and love of travel and culture led her the steppes of Mongolia. Hannah was a United States Peace Corps volunteer in the capital city of Ulaanbaatar from 2009-2011. She was a methodology and language instructor at Gazarchin University. During her time in Central Asia, Hannah travelled to China, Thailand, and Cambodia. After successfully completing her Peace Corps service, Hannah moved to New Orleans to compete her Master’s degree in public administration and nonprofit leadership at the nationally-ranked Public Administration program at the University of New Orleans. While attending graduate school, Hannah was originally the office coordinator and was then promoted to director of leasing for a multinational student housing company.

Hannah Maharrey lives in Tupelo, Mississippi area and works within the nonprofit sector in education management and workforce development. She is also active within her community by supporting fellow nonprofits through volunteering, fundraising, and advocacy.
